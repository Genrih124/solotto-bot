How to Use Phantom Wallet for On-Chain Games in 2026

📅 April 2026 ⏱ 8 min read ✍️ SOLOTTO Research

Phantom is the gateway to Solana's on-chain ecosystem. It is the wallet that connects your identity — your public key — to every dApp, game, and protocol on the network. Without it, you cannot interact with smart contracts, buy tickets, claim prizes, or do anything that requires an on-chain signature.

The good news is that setting up Phantom and using it for on-chain games takes about five minutes and requires no technical knowledge. This guide walks you through every step — from installation to your first on-chain game transaction — and covers the security practices that will keep your funds safe long term.

What Is Phantom and Why Does It Matter?

Phantom is a non-custodial wallet — meaning it never holds your funds. Instead, it stores your private key locally on your device and uses it to sign transactions on your behalf. When you click "Buy Ticket" on SOLOTTO, Phantom constructs the transaction, shows you exactly what it will do, and asks for your approval before broadcasting it to the Solana network.

This approval step is fundamental to how on-chain applications work. No dApp can move your funds without a transaction signed by your private key. Phantom is the gatekeeper — and because it is non-custodial, even Phantom itself cannot access your funds without your approval. This is the core security model of self-custody on Solana.

Step-by-Step: Setting Up Phantom

1

Install the Phantom Extension

Go to phantom.com and download the browser extension for Chrome, Firefox, Brave, or Edge. On mobile, download the Phantom app from the App Store or Google Play. Always download from the official website or app store — never from a third-party link.

2

Create a New Wallet

Open Phantom and select "Create a new wallet." Phantom will generate a seed phrase — 12 or 24 random words that are the master key to your wallet. Write these words down on paper in the exact order shown. Do not take a screenshot. Do not store them digitally. This phrase is the only way to recover your wallet if you lose your device.

3

Set a Password

Create a strong password for the Phantom app on your device. This password locks the app locally — it is not your seed phrase and cannot recover your wallet. It just prevents someone with physical access to your device from opening Phantom without your permission.

4

Fund Your Wallet with SOL

To interact with Solana dApps, you need SOL. You can buy SOL directly within Phantom using a credit or debit card via the built-in on-ramp, or transfer SOL from a centralized exchange like Coinbase or Binance to your Phantom wallet address. Your wallet address is shown at the top of the Phantom interface — it starts with a string of letters and numbers unique to your wallet.

5

Connect to a dApp

Navigate to a Solana dApp — like SOLOTTO at solotto.cc. Click "Connect Wallet." Phantom will pop up and ask you to approve the connection. Connecting does not give the dApp any access to your funds — it only lets the dApp see your public wallet address. No transaction can occur without you explicitly approving it in Phantom.

6

Sign Your First Transaction

When you take an action that requires an on-chain transaction — like buying a ticket in SOLOTTO — Phantom will show you a transaction preview. This preview displays exactly what the transaction will do: which program it calls, how much SOL will be transferred, and what the estimated network fee is. Review this carefully, then click Approve to sign and broadcast the transaction.

💡 Transaction fees on Solana are tiny. A typical Phantom transaction costs between $0.00010 and $0.00050 in SOL — less than a fraction of a cent. You will never encounter the $5–50 gas fees associated with Ethereum transactions.

Understanding What Phantom Shows You

Before approving any transaction, Phantom shows a preview that includes the program being called, the accounts involved, and the SOL amount being transferred. For on-chain games like SOLOTTO, this preview will show you exactly how much SOL you are sending to the room's prize vault — nothing hidden, nothing unexpected.

If a transaction preview shows something you did not expect — funds going to an unfamiliar address, permissions you did not intend to grant — reject it immediately. Legitimate dApps never ask for more than what is needed for the specific action you initiated.

Security Best Practices for On-Chain Gaming

Protect Your Seed Phrase Above Everything

Your seed phrase is the master key to your wallet. Anyone who has it can import your wallet on any device and access all of your funds. Store it on paper, in a safe location, offline. Never type it into any website, app, or chat — legitimate services never ask for your seed phrase.

🚨 The number one scam vector: Fake "support" accounts on Twitter and Discord that direct users to websites asking for their seed phrase. Phantom support will never ask for your seed phrase. No legitimate dApp will ever ask for it. If someone asks — it is a scam, always.

Use a Dedicated Gaming Wallet

Create a separate Phantom wallet for on-chain gaming and keep only the SOL you intend to use in that session. Your main holdings stay in a separate wallet that you never connect to dApps. This limits your exposure if you accidentally approve a malicious transaction.

Verify Contract Addresses

Before interacting with any dApp, verify that the contract address shown in Phantom matches the address published on the project's official website. For SOLOTTO, the contract address is 8djAC69852xokSdr3joE18eMKVNHT5jPggpHidkYLngA — verifiable on Solscan and on this site. If the address does not match, do not proceed.

Review Every Transaction

Never auto-approve Phantom transactions. Read the preview carefully every time. Legitimate games call known program addresses with expected SOL amounts. If anything looks unfamiliar, reject and investigate before approving.

Using Phantom on Mobile

Phantom's mobile app includes a built-in browser that lets you access Solana dApps directly without switching between apps. Open the browser tab inside Phantom, navigate to solotto.cc, and the wallet connection happens automatically within the same app. Prize receipts and transaction history are visible in the Phantom activity feed in real time.

Mobile Phantom also supports Face ID and fingerprint authentication for transaction signing, making the approve flow faster than typing a password while maintaining security. For frequent on-chain game players, mobile is often the most convenient experience.

Troubleshooting Common Phantom Issues

Transaction Failing

If a transaction fails, the most common cause is insufficient SOL for the network fee. Solana requires a small amount of SOL to be held in every active account as rent — ensure your wallet has slightly more SOL than the ticket price you are attempting to buy.

Wallet Not Connecting

If Phantom does not pop up when you click Connect Wallet, try refreshing the page, disabling other wallet extensions that might conflict, or switching to a different browser. Phantom works best in Chrome and Brave.

Transaction Stuck as Pending

Solana transactions occasionally get dropped during periods of high network load. If your transaction shows as pending for more than 30 seconds, it has likely been dropped — check your wallet balance to confirm no SOL was deducted, then retry. The Solana network self-resolves dropped transactions without any action needed on your part.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Phantom wallet free to use? ▼
Yes. Phantom wallet is completely free to download and use. The only costs are Solana network transaction fees, which are typically a fraction of a cent per transaction.
Can I use Phantom wallet on mobile? ▼
Yes. Phantom is available as a mobile app for both iOS and Android, with a built-in browser that lets you access Solana dApps directly from your phone without needing a desktop computer.
What happens if I lose access to my Phantom wallet? ▼
Your recovery phrase (seed phrase) is the only way to restore access. Store it securely offline — written on paper in a safe location. Anyone with your seed phrase can access your funds, so never share it with anyone under any circumstances.
Does connecting Phantom to a dApp give it access to my funds? ▼
No. Connecting your wallet only lets the dApp see your public wallet address. No funds can be moved without you explicitly approving a transaction in Phantom. Always review transaction previews before approving.
How much SOL do I need to play SOLOTTO? ▼
The minimum room is 0.05 SOL per ticket. You also need a tiny amount extra for network fees — typically less than $0.001. Having 0.1 SOL in your wallet gives you comfortable room to play the smallest rooms multiple times.
